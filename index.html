About Us

We are partnering with the UCSD chapter of the Food Recovery Network (FRN), a national nonprofit dedicated to reducing food waste and fighting food insecurity. Our local FRN chapter shares this mission by working both on and off campus to support UCSD students facing food insecurity.

To further these efforts, we are developing a community garden near the FRN office. This garden will provide fresh herbs, vegetables, and other nutritious foods to the UCSD community, creating a sustainable and easily accessible food source. Beyond addressing food insecurity, the garden will also promote environmental sustainability by encouraging eco-friendly growing practices and fostering a deeper connection between students and the food they consume.

With thoughtful planning and intentional design, the garden will serve as a valuable resource for FRN visitors while also attracting new students and staff. By cultivating fresh produce on campus, we hope to strengthen food security, promote sustainability, and foster a stronger sense of community at UCSD.
